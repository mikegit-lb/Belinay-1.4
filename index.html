Lesson Overview

1. Lesson Goals

Speaking Goals

Belinay can describe a role model, give reasons, tell a short life story, and explain what makes someone inspiring.

Language Goals

Use character adjectives, relative clauses with who, past simple, present perfect, and should / shouldn’t.

Safe speaking rule: You do not have to share private family problems or personal details. You can talk about a teacher, celebrity, athlete, fictional character, or a general family role model.

Grammar Support

3. Target Grammar

Relative Clauses with “who”

Use who to describe a person.

Structure: A role model is someone who + verb + object/complement.

✅ A role model is someone who helps others.

✅ I admire people who stay calm.

✅ A good celebrity is someone who uses fame responsibly.

❌ A role model is someone helps people.

✅ A role model is someone who helps people.

Turkish idea: “yardım eden kişi” → English needs who.

Past Simple for Life Stories

Use past simple for finished events in the past.

last year two years ago when she was younger in 2022

She started a kindness club.

He grew up in a small town.

They raised money for poor families last year.

Present Perfect for Achievements

Use present perfect for achievements and life experiences when the exact time is not the main focus.

Structure: has / have + V3

Past SimplePresent Perfect
She started a project in 2021.She has helped many students.
He won an award last year.He has won three awards.
My aunt studied medicine.My aunt has supported many patients.

Should / Shouldn’t for Role Model Behavior

Use should to say what is good, right, or responsible.

✅ Role models should be honest.

✅ Celebrities should use their influence carefully.

❌ He should to be kind.

✅ He should be kind.

Modal + base verb. No “to”.

7. Reading Text: An Inspiring Young Person

The Teenager Who Made a Difference

Maya is a 16-year-old student from a small city. When she was younger, she noticed that some students at her school felt lonely during breaks. Some students were shy, and others had just moved to the city. They didn’t know how to make friends.

At first, Maya didn’t know what to do. She was not a famous person, and she didn’t think she could change anything. But one day, she saw a new student eating lunch alone. Maya sat next to her and started a conversation. After that, she had an idea.

Maya started a small school club called “Sit With Us.” The idea was simple: no student should feel invisible. Club members invited lonely students to join games, lunch tables, and group activities. In the beginning, only five students joined the club. After a few months, more than thirty students became members.

Maya has helped many students feel more confident. She has also spoken at a school meeting about kindness and friendship. Last year, her school gave her a kindness award. Maya said, “You don’t have to be famous to be a role model. Sometimes, you only need to notice someone.”

Maya is inspiring because she is thoughtful, brave, and determined. She shows that everyday kindness can make a real difference.

Speaking Structure

9. Role Model Description Framework

Use this framework to prepare a clear B1-level answer.

1. Introduction
My role model is ________. I look up to this person because ________.
2. Identity
He/She is someone who ________. He/She is known for ________.
3. Character
I think this person is ________, ________, and ________.
4. Life Story
When he/she was younger, he/she ________. He/She faced ________, but he/she ________.
5. Achievements
He/She has ________. He/She has helped/inspired ________.
6. Lesson
This person teaches me that ________. I think we should ________.
Show B1 Model Answer

My role model is my older cousin. I look up to her because she is hardworking and supportive. She is someone who always tries to help people around her.

When she was younger, she studied a lot because she wanted to become a doctor. She faced many challenges, but she didn’t give up. She has passed difficult exams and has helped many people in her hospital.

I think she is determined, patient, and kind-hearted. She teaches me that success is not only about talent. It is also about effort and responsibility. I believe role models should be honest and helpful, but they shouldn’t act like they are better than other people.

11. Homework

Part 1: Vocabulary

Choose 10 words from the vocabulary list and write one sentence for each.

Example: My sister is determined because she never gives up.

Part 2: Writing

Write 120–150 words about a role model.

Use 5 vocabulary words, 2 sentences with who, 2 past simple sentences, 2 present perfect sentences, and 2 should/shouldn’t sentences.

Part 3: Speaking

Prepare a 1-minute speech titled Someone Who Inspires Me.

Practice once slowly, then once naturally.
